Lac Courte Oreilles Tribal Code of Law.

ELE.1.5.010 Caucus

(a) The Caucus shall be held in the month of April of each year, on a date designated by the Tribal Governing Board. At the Caucus, the Tribal Governing Board shall receive nominations of eligible members of the Tribe for vacancies on the Tribal Governing Board and election which shall be held in the month of June of an election year. Also, at the Election Caucus, the nomination and election of the Election Committee and Election Observers shall take place.

(b) Election Caucus Procedure.

(1) Sign-in sheet available at entrance to the location designated by the Tribal Governing Board to hold the Election Caucus.

(2) Call to order — Roll call.

(3) Floor opened for nominations of eligible members of the Tribe for election.

(A) Present name of nominator.

(B) Nominator names nominee.

(4) Chairman calls three (3) times for any additional nominees before floor is closed for additional nominations.

(5) Names of nominees are placed in a "hat" and drawn out for numeral order on the voting ballot. If a nominee withdraws, then the remainder of nominees shall move up in that order.

(6) Floor opens for nominations to fill six (6) positions on the Election Committee.

(A) Present name of nominator.

(B) Nominator names nominee.

(7) Chairman calls three (3) times for any additional nominees before floor is closed for additional nominations.

(c) Election Committee Voting Procedures. Secretaries shall pass out ballots to all enrolled tribal members present.

(1) Only six (6) votes per ballot. If for any reason the ballot contains more than six (6) votes per ballot, then ballot shall be considered "spoiled" and cast out.

(2) The ballots shall be collected at each table.

(3) The ballots shall be counted and cross referenced with the sign-in sheet to estimate numbers.

(A) Ballots shall be tallied and marked on display chart.

(B) Six (6) top vote getters are seated on Election Committee.

(C) Two (2) next highest vote getters are designated as alternates.

(d) Election Observers Voting Procedures. Secretaries shall pass out ballots to all enrolled tribal members present.

(1) The number of Observers for an election is the same number of offices to be filled in the election, i.e., three (3) or four (4). If for any reason the ballot contains more than three (3) or four (4) votes per ballot, the ballot shall be considered "spoiled" and cast out.

(2) The ballots shall be collected at each table.

(3) The ballots shall be counted and cross referenced with the sign-in sheet to estimate numbers.

(A) Ballots shall be tallied and marked on display chart.

(B) Three (3) or Four (4) top vote getters are seated as Election Observers.

(C) Two (2) next highest vote getters are designated as alternates.

(e) Chairman adjourns Caucus.

(Res. No. 20-2021; Res. No. 19-39; Res. No. 17-53.)
